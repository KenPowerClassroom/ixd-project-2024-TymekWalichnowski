Competitive Audit

Question Scratch Kahoot Typing.com
Engagement: How engaging is the app? Is it visually appealing? Does it have sound and animation to capture the user's attention? Does it have a good balance of challenge and reward? Scratch is very engaging due to the fact that the user can make their own games very easily, there are no built-in sounds or animations but the site itself and editor is very visually appealing with how blocks of code are put together. (8/10) Kahoot is incredibly engaging due to the way it uses gamificiation elements, students must engage in competitive quizes with one another. Answering faster gets more points, the music is very catchy and build tension and it is aesthetically pleasing with its simple aesthetics. (9/10) The app is engaging due to how it gamifies typing, it uses typing sounds and animation to capture the user's attention? and it has a good balance of increasing challenge that makes users want to improve, along with having "exp" and "levels" to incentivise use. (9/10)
Progress tracking: How well does the app track the user's progress? Does it provide feedback and recommendations to help users improve their performance? Does it use data to personalize the learning experience Scratch doesn't track user progress or provide feedback, nor does it use data to personalize the learning experience. It is hands-off and up to the user to track their own progress by looking at their own gallery. (2/10) A total leaderboard is shown at the end of quizes but there is no way to track your progress, nor does it give feedback to the user on how to improve. (1/10) The application does an excellent job at tracking user progress with the exp and level system, showcasing a user's general progress, while the Average Typing Speed, Accuracy and Time stats act as very strong goal-posts. It does not have any personalised plans, however. (8/10)
User experience: How user-friendly is the app? Is it easy to navigate and use? Does it have clear instructions and prompts? Does it have a well-designed interface? Scratch is very user-friendly as it is able to simplify something as complicated as coding into a very simple block/puzzle formula that is very clear on how it works. The interface is also clear and easy to understand. It could use some built-in tutorials, however. (8/10) The app is very easy to use, hence why it is such a staple in classroom environments. There are clear instructions and prompts for the students and they should know exactly what to do at any moment. (10/10) The website is simple and easy to navigate with clear instructions and prompts, it is also aesthetically pleasing. (9/10)
